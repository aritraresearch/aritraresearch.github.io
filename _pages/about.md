I am a PhD research scholar and academic researcher specializing in the application of artificial intelligence to sustainable agro-ecosystems. My doctoral work focuses on integrating soil classification, carbon sequestration modeling, and precision agriculture. Rather than relying on localized field-based data acquisition, my computational modeling utilizes robust, publicly available datasets to build scalable AI solutions. 

My technical research involves rigorous comparative assessments of advanced deep learning architectures, particularly evaluating Convolutional Neural Networks against Vision Transformers (such as Swin-B) for complex image-based classification tasks. Because transparency is critical in deployed AI, I prioritize model interpretability by deeply integrating Explainable AI (XAI) techniques—including SHAP, LIME, and Grad-CAM—into my workflows for sensitivity analysis, bias detection, and visual validation. 

Beyond agricultural applications, my research interests extend into next-generation computational paradigms. This includes recent work in Energy-Aware Federated Edge Intelligence, Green Federated Learning (Green-FL), and utilizing intersectional frameworks to mitigate patriarchal biases in Large Language Models. 

In addition to my research, I serve as an educator and project supervisor in the fields of cloud computing and machine intelligence, where I help guide the next generation of developers and researchers.
